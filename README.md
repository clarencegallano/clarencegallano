I'm Clarence, a rising junior at the University of Hawai'i at Mānoa, majoring in Finance — and what excites me most isn't just where markets stand today, but how they got there. I love digging into the histories of businesses: unpacking the strategies that made them thrive, the missteps that brought them down, and the ripple effects that still shape the global economy now. That lens gives me a richer, more grounded way to approach financial analysis — one built on context and long-term thinking, not just spreadsheets and snapshots.

I've also built a diverse toolkit through hands-on experience: as an office assistant, I sharpened my organizational instincts and attention to detail; as an art gallery attendant, I learned to connect with people, tell a compelling story, and communicate clearly in a fast-paced, client-facing setting.

Now, I'm channeling all of that into my next big step: landing a internship at a bank, where I can put my financial knowledge to work in the real world. And I'm not stopping there — down the road, I'm committed to pursuing a post-graduate degree to deepen my expertise and establish myself as a credible, driven voice in the finance industry.
